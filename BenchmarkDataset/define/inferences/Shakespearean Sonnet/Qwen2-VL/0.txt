A Shakespearean sonnet is a type of sonnet that was popularized by the English playwright and poet William Shakespeare. It consists of 14 lines, with a rhyme scheme of ABAB CDCD EFEF GG. The first 12 lines are made up of three quatrains (four-line stanzas), and the last two lines are a rhyming couplet. The sonnet typically explores a single theme or idea, and often uses metaphors and other literary devices to convey its meaning.
